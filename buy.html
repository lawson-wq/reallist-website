Buying journey

Understand the process before you enter the market.

1. Finance ready

Bank approval tells you what you may borrow. It does not tell you what you should borrow.

2. Buying structure

Investors should discuss ownership structure, tax questions and long-term plans before buying, not after the numbers become painful.

3. Search criteria

Define area, budget, bedrooms, school zone, risk tolerance and deal-breakers before the photos start doing their little emotional tricks.

4. Due diligence

Check title, LIM, property file, consent history, insurance, inspection and risk before making an expensive mistake.

5. Offer

A rushed offer is not a strong offer. A clear offer is.

6. Settlement & ownership

Confirm insurance, finance, solicitor review, settlement timing and post-purchase management before you relax.

Buyer guides

Useful, not decorative.

First-home buyer guide

Check finance, deposit, documents, condition, insurance and offer conditions before you buy.

  • A beautiful kitchen does not fix a leaking roof.
  • If you cannot insure it properly, you may not be able to settle it properly.

Investor guide

Check rent, yield, repayment, tax structure questions, holding costs and vacancy risk.

  • Investment property should be tested by numbers, not emotions.
  • A property can look good and still fail the cashflow.

Land & development guide

Check zoning, services, flooding, access, infrastructure capacity and consultant feasibility before relying on development potential.

  • Potential is not approval.
  • Maps help you ask better questions, not replace professional advice.
